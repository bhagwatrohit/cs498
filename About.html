This dashboard is created by Rohit Bhagwat as a part of the final project submission for the course: CS498 Data Visualization at University of Illinois, Urbana Champaign.

In the past few weeks of the course we got to learn about the power of D3 visualizations. D3 is a powerful Javascript library which helps developers to associate HTML elements with data. I was very new to the world of Javascript and D3, so I enjoyed this course a lot.

As a part of this project, I decided to visualize player performances in La-Liga (Spanish Football league) for the past 9 years. In those years we have witnessed two of the greatest soccer players to have played the game play every week in La Liga - Lionel Messi and Cristiano Ronaldo. I wanted to visualize their impact to the league through the numbers.

I collected the data for the past 9 years from http://whoscored.com.
For the purposes of this dashboard, I’m only concentrating on the Goals Scored and Assists provided by the players (not particularly good parameters for a defensive or a midfield player, but we’ll stick to it for this visualization).
In order to concentrate on data visualization, I have pre-prepared the data and stored in CSV files. For each year we will be looking at the Top 20 players only.

I’m providing answers to the criteria in the Rubric in the below table for simple reading.

Criteria
Description
Does the narrative visualization correctly follow the hybrid structure as stated by the essay?
In this dashboard I am using the Interactive Slideshow hybrid structure.
The reason for this is it enables us to browse through the player performances in a consistent manner across visualizations and particularly let us focus on Lionel Messi
Does the narrative visualization effectively utilize scenes?
I’m following a consistent scene throughout the interactive journey. The focus is on comparing the performances between two players across multiple years while maintaining the consistency in the view. I focused on a single chart (scatterplot) in a single scene.
Does the narrative visualization effectively utilize annotations?
The primary purpose of the visualization is to focus on the performance of these two players. I’m using annotations to annotate their performances through the years.
Does the narrative visualization effectively utilize parameters?
There are two parameters - parameter to change the years and mouseover parameter which enables the user to hover and look into the details for each player.
Does the narrative visualization effectively utilize triggers?
The year buttons let the user change the years which server as triggers for the scene.
